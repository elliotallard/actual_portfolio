Queer Abstraction: Then & Now







In this piece, I present queer abstraction as seen in various artists - its implementation in queer art, the subjects these artists represented (in some cases), and my own understanding of the artworks. I use Andy Warhol's Ladies & Gentlemen series to open the study, and tie in the pieces of Gordon Hall and Felix Gonzalez-Torres, as well as the writing of David Getsy and Darby English to shine a light on abstraction in the queer visual language.

for the class Queer Art & Culture
taught by Suzie Silver and Jamison Edgar
